The Basics

Understanding the fundamentals of fantasy football

Fantasy football is a game where you act as the owner and manager of your own virtual football team. Your team competes against other managers' teams based on the statistical performance of the players in real-world matches.

How to Play

To get started with PlayGroundStrategy, follow these simple steps:

  1. Create an Account: Sign up for a free account on PlayGroundStrategy.
  2. Join or Create a League: Join an existing public league or create your own private league and invite friends.
  3. Draft Your Team: Select real-world players to form your fantasy team during the draft.
  4. Set Your Lineup: Before each matchday, set your active lineup from your roster.
  5. Score Points: Your players earn points based on their real-world performance.
  6. Manage Your Team: Throughout the season, make trades, pick up free agents, and adjust your lineup to maximize points.
  7. Win Your League: Compete head-to-head with other managers and aim to win your league championship.

Draft Rules

How the player selection process works

The draft is one of the most exciting parts of fantasy football. It's where you select the players who will make up your team for the season. PlayGroundStrategy offers several draft formats:

Draft Formats

Snake Draft

The draft order reverses each round. If you pick first in round 1, you'll pick last in round 2, and so on.

Auction Draft

Each manager has a budget to bid on players. The highest bidder wins each player until all rosters are filled.

Live Draft

All managers draft simultaneously in real-time, with a timer for each selection.

Auto Draft

The system automatically selects players for you based on pre-rankings or your draft list.

Draft Preparation

To prepare for your draft:

  • Research players and create a draft list with your preferred rankings.
  • Set up tiers for each position to help make decisions during the draft.
  • Consider positional scarcity - some positions have fewer elite players available.
  • Don't forget about bye weeks - avoid having too many players on bye in the same week.
  • Use the PlayGroundStrategy mock draft tool to practice different strategies.

Roster Management

How to manage your team throughout the season

Effective roster management is crucial for fantasy football success. Here's what you need to know:

Standard Roster Composition

The standard fantasy football team consists of:

  • 1 Quarterback (QB)
  • 2 Running Backs (RB)
  • 2 Wide Receivers (WR)
  • 1 Tight End (TE)
  • 1 Flex (RB/WR/TE)
  • 1 Team Defense/Special Teams (D/ST)
  • 1 Kicker (K)
  • 6 Bench spots
  • 1 Injured Reserve (IR) spot

League commissioners can customize this format for their leagues.

Setting Your Lineup

Before each matchday, you must set your active lineup by selecting which players will earn points for your team. Players on your bench do not earn points for your team that week.

Lineup Lock Times

Lineups lock at the start time of each player's real-world match. Make sure to adjust your lineup before kickoff!

Trades & Waivers

How to acquire new players and make trades

Throughout the season, you can improve your team by making trades with other managers or picking up free agents through the waiver system.

Trading System

The trading system allows managers to exchange players with each other. Here's how it works:

  • Proposing Trades: You can propose trades to any other manager in your league.
  • Review Period: All trades are subject to a 24-hour review period.
  • Trade Vetoes: Leagues can be set up with commissioner approval or league vote veto systems.
  • Trade Deadlines: Trades are typically locked a few weeks before playoffs begin.

Waiver Wire System

The waiver wire is how you acquire free agents (players not on any team in your league):

  • Waiver Priority: Each manager is assigned a waiver priority ranking, with #1 being the highest priority.
  • Waiver Period: When a player is dropped, they typically go on waivers for 24-48 hours.
  • FAAB System: Some leagues use a Free Agent Acquisition Budget (FAAB) where managers bid on free agents.
  • Waiver Claims: Submit your claims before the waiver deadline, and they'll process according to priority or highest bid.

Pro Tip

Pay attention to your waiver priority. In some leagues, using a high waiver claim moves you to the bottom of the priority list, so use it wisely!

League Types

Different formats of fantasy football leagues

PlayGroundStrategy offers several types of fantasy football leagues to suit different playing styles and preferences:

League Formats

Standard Leagues

The traditional head-to-head format where you face off against a different opponent each week.

Points Leagues

Focus purely on total points scored throughout the season rather than weekly matchups.

Dynasty Leagues

Multi-year leagues where you keep most or all of your players from season to season.

Custom Leagues

Create your own league with custom scoring, roster settings, and rules.

League Settings

When creating or joining a league, consider these important settings:

  • Scoring Type: Standard, PPR (point per reception), or Half-PPR.
  • League Size: Typically 8-14 teams, with 10-12 being most common.
  • Playoffs: Usually weeks 14-16 or 15-17 of the NFL season, with 4, 6, or 8 teams qualifying.
  • Keeper Rules: If applicable, how many players can be kept between seasons and at what cost.
  • Public vs Private: Public leagues are open to anyone, while private leagues require an invitation.

Important Note

League settings should be established before the draft and typically cannot be changed once the season has begun.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about fantasy football rules

What happens if a player gets injured during a game?

If a player gets injured during a game, you will receive points for any statistics they accumulated before the injury. You cannot substitute a player once their game has begun.

Can I change my lineup after games have started?

You can only change players in your lineup whose games have not yet started. Once a player's game begins, they are locked in their current position (active or bench) for that week.

What happens in case of a tie during playoffs?

In case of a tie during playoffs, the default tiebreaker is the higher seed advances. League commissioners can also set custom tiebreakers such as bench points, quarterback points, or regular season head-to-head record.

Can I drop a player who has already played this week?

Yes, you can drop a player who has already played this week, but they will go on waivers and you won't be able to pick them up again until the waiver period has cleared.

What is the difference between PPR and Standard scoring?

In PPR (Points Per Reception) leagues, players receive 1 point for each reception they make. In Standard scoring, no points are awarded for receptions. This increases the value of players who catch many passes, such as receiving running backs and slot receivers.

How are playoff teams determined?

Playoff teams are typically determined by win-loss record, with tiebreakers being total points scored, head-to-head record, or division record (if applicable). The number of playoff teams and seeding structure can be customized by the league commissioner.
